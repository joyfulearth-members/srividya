# Day 9 – Mātaṅgī: The Wild Voice of Truth

🔹 Sanskrit Verse

```sanskritized-english
Maharajyeem dhanavantrireem
Mavanyadevimatangeem
Twambhavani aranyeshwaree
Pakshmimatreem sumeghadhatum
Neelabhadri neelakshi
Shukahhastey veenamdhari
Satyachiturja somaamritey
Bhikshupatrey navakhyitey
RishiputriMatangi aranyalatikavimoshani
Durbhagyasahitaprani twamdrishistanam
Danditabhedvishesh kalpanasurichati
Paramsanskaradevi
Oṁ Hrīṁ Śrīṁ Mātaṅgyai Namaḥ.
```

---

🔸 Poetic Translation

```poetic-translation
You dwell in the green jungle of sounds,
Mātaṅgī, untamed daughter of speech.
Parrot in hand, wisdom on tongue,
You sing beyond caste, creed, and containment.
You break all refined rules with honeyed truth,
Feeding outcasts and sovereigns alike.
You are the voice I silenced too long—
Now I let it rise.
```

---

✨ Inner Meaning

```inner-meaning
Mātaṅgī is the goddess of inner voice and rejected knowledge. She embodies wild wisdom,
creative flow, and marginalized truth. She empowers artists, tantrikas, and rebels. Her presence
opens throat and third eye—especially for those whose voices were once silenced.
```
