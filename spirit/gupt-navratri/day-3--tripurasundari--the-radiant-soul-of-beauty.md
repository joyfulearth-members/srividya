# Day 3 – Tripurasundarī: The Radiant Soul of Beauty

🔹 Sanskrit Verse

```sanskritized-english
Chandramauli dasharasaam
Maalalitatripurasundari
Shobhinyam kaavyam garudam
Kamalapadeshwari chandreem
Chakileshabhuva vilvastitham
Charanakavyam samarpitaam
Balavidyam parivarthaney
Hasyagunitham kritakshareem
Sribalatripurasundarideiveem
TwamevajanmamNamostute
Kritirupena dharma rakshitaam

Tavapada jyotir samarpayami
Saumyalakshita SriLalithatripurasundari
Twamgranthiatmaswarupini
Vidyadayini sukhapradaini divyashaktivimarshini krodhanashini
Srimaata gyaneshwari navapravesha uddharini
Namah SrijyotiHreem samarpayaami Shreem
```

---

🔸 Poetic Translation

```poetic-translation
O Tripurasundarī, who wears the moon like poetry,
You are the grace of laughter, of wisdom’s first light.
Your lotus feet hold verses the earth cannot forget.
I lay down my childish cleverness at your jeweled threshold.
You transform intellect into devotion,
Kindling joy where ego once lived.
Goddess of all knowledge and delight,
I surrender to your radiant syllables, Hreem and Shreem.
```

---

✨ Inner Meaning

```inner-meaning
She is the third-eye light—beauty that awakens wisdom. Tripurasundarī governs the ajñā chakra
and invites us to abandon false intellect and melt into intuitive knowing. She is the laughter
behind creation, the impulse of art, the tranquil power of self-realization. Her mantra is soft,
fragrant, piercing.
```
