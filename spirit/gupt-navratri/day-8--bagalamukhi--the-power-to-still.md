# Day 8 – Bagalāmukhī: The Power to Still

🔹 Sanskrit Verse

```sanskritized-english
Angarakha niveshetu
Bagalamukhi pravesheti
Jeevajantu sampanney
Janmasushobhita sugandheem
Bhavanadharam stambeyitinetro
Matsyabhojana navivekamanasee

Naprarambha naantakaley
Vimushya parijantu naprabhavey
Maabagala twamsuryaatiswarnamukhey
Muma atmanam stambiteydevi
Nidrabhogajayam napranadeitey
Twamvikalpitanayano aasirwadadehidey
Oṁ Hlīṁ Bagalāmukhyai Namaḥ.
```

<!--more-->

---

🔸 Poetic Translation

```poetic-translation
Golden-tongued goddess, silencer of chaos,
You freeze the false tongue and halt all harm.
Bagalāmukhī, your gaze arrests the storm.
Even time pauses at your stillness.
Where distraction ruled, you plant presence.
Where anxiety danced, you whisper: stop.
I surrender my scattered will to you—
Anchor me in golden calm.
```

---

✨ Inner Meaning

```inner-meaning
Bagalāmukhī is the power of stambhana, the stilling of distortion. She grants mastery over
speech, illusion, and aggressive energy. Her worship is especially potent in moments of legal
battles, gossip, and self-doubt. She is deep pause and focused will.
```
