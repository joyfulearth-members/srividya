# Day 4 – Bhuvaneśvarī: The Womb of Worlds

🔹 Sanskrit Verse

```sanskritized-english
Navanatyarambhey bhaveshi
Navagraharasamanamohini

Shaambhavena samasta triputa tripureshwari
Swarnamayurarupini Divya vyakheem
Trikalanetreem mahakaalasakheem
Mangalabhavishya prapteem
Dhanyamdhanam swaroopini
SriShodashisundari Deiveem kleem namostute
Maatamjayaam bhuvanaprastutey
MaaBhuvaneshwari jagadambal
Pritvisanjana prasanna praveshi
Nrityaspandana taalam nidhi
Swetakalika nityaranjini
Veeryojana atmadarshati
Mayurakhyo pravachana samarpayami
Mabhuvanesvarikalyani
Devijwalitha vachana nirmanee
Agnijaladhatusangami
Muktanivesha api Jayam jayati
```

---

🔸 Poetic Translation

```poetic-translation
Mother of movement and measure, O Bhuvaneśvarī,
You dance in the breath of planets and time.
Your voice, like a white peacock, summons the unseen—
All of creation listens and sways.
You enter the earth through the beat of joy.
You are wealth, destiny, spacious truth.

I offer my words to your golden tongue—
May your presence crown all beginnings.
```

✨ Inner Meaning

```inner-meaning
She is the goddess of expansive space, the ākāśa tattva. Bhuvaneśvarī fills the heart with
rhythm, voice, and vastness. She aligns the seeker with divine timing and destiny. Through her,
sound becomes speech, desire becomes dance. A true invocation of divine orchestration.
```
